Eminem has released eleven studio albums, two compilation albums, and one extended play. His production and vocals were also featured in two soundtrack and collaborative albums. In addition, Eminem released 57 singles as a lead artist, and was featured in 22 singles as a guest artist. Overall, he has sold around 220 million records worldwide, as well as 227.5 million certified albums and singles in the U.S.

Studio Albums: Infinite (1996); The Slim Shady LP (1999); The Marshall Mathers LP (2000); The Eminem Show (2002); Encore (2004); Relapse (2009); Recovery (2010); The Marshall Mathers LP 2 (2013); Revival (2017); Kamikaze (2018); Music to Be Murdered By (2020);
Compilation Albums: Curtain Call: The Hits (2005); Curtain Call 2 (2022);
Extended Plays: Slim Shady EP (1997);
Collaborative Albums: Eminem Presents: The Re-Up (2006); Shady XV (2014);
Soundtrack Albums: 8 Mile (2002); Southpaw (2015);

Infinite (1996)

Infinite is Eminem's debut studio album that was released on November 12, 1996. Prior to its release, Eminem began working on the album due to his inspiration of artists like Tupac Shakur and Nas. The album's physical copies were released on both cassette and vinyl, which he sold copies from the trunk of his car. After selling only 1,000 copies, it was a commercial faliure and received mixed reviews from critics. Despite being not officially available on any online music stores, one of the album's single, "Infinite", was the only track that was available on Spotify.

The Slim Shady LP (1999)

The Slim Shady LP is Eminem's second studio album that was released on February 23, 1999. It was considered to be a major label debut due to Eminem's signing with Aftermath Entertainment and Interscope Records. This album contains cartoonish depictions of violence and despite the lyrics being satrical, it was most likely described as a horror film-esque. Nevertheless, it peaked at number two on the Billboard 200 and was a commercial success. Critics have praised the album's singles for its unique lyrical style, dark humor, and odd personality.

The Marshall Mathers LP (2000)

The Marshall Mathers LP is Eminem's third studio album that was released on May 30, 2000. It features more introspective lyricism, which includes Eminem's opinions on his rise to becoming rich, criticism of his music, and his family's estrangement. Although the album's lyrics were criticized for being violent, homophobic, and misogynistic, The Marshall Mathers LP debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, staying atop for eight consecutive weeks. Thus, it sold 1.78 million copies in its first week, making it the fastest-selling albums in the U.S.

The Eminem Show (2002)

The Eminem Show is Eminem's fourth studio album that was released on May 26, 2002. It incorporates a heavy use of rap rock and its themes are primarily based on Eminem's prominence in hip-hop, as well as his uncertain thoughts of popularity. The album also features political references such as 9/11 attacks, Osama bin Laden, George W. Bush, and Tipper Gore.

Encore (2004)

Encore is Eminem's fifth studio album that was released on November 12, 2004. The album contains several lyrical themes like his opposition towards George W. Bush and a parody towards Michael Jackson.

Relapse (2009) and Recovery (2010)

Relapse is his sixth studio album that was released on May 15, 2009. It revolves around horror, drug rehabilitation, and relapse. After receiving mixed reviews from critics, Eminem released Recovery during the following year. Unlike its prdecessor, Recovery features more emotional content that revolves around positive changes, anxiety, and emotional drives.

The Marshall Mathers LP 2 (2013)

The Marshall Mathers LP 2 is Eminem's eighth studio album that was released on November 5, 2013. It serves as a sequel towards his third studio album, The Marshall Mathers LP. It draws influences from arena rock and old school hip-hop that was inspired by LL Cool J's Radio era.

Revival (2017), Kamikaze (2018) and Music to Be Murdered By (2020)

Revival is his ninth studio album that was released on December 15, 2017. It contains many lines critiquing Donald Trump's campaign and election. Since it has received mixed reviews regarding with its rock-influenced production, Eminem released Kamikaze during the following year. The album represents a continuation of Revival's response, containing insults towards critics and other rappers alike. His most recent album, Music to Be Murdered By, was released on January 17, 2020.
